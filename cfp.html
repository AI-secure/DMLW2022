Call for Papers

We invite submissions that have at most 6 pages with unlimited bibliography and appendix.

Topics include but are not limited to:

  • Theoretical understanding of trustworthy machine learning and blockchains
  • Trustworthy ML systems via decentralization and blockchains
  • Decentralized AI systems for real-world deployment
  • Decentralized Data management in blockchain and AI systems
  • Distributed Consensus & Fault Tolerance Algorithms
  • Blockchain for Internet of Things/Cyber Physical Services
  • Adversarial attacks against ML systems, including training and test time attacks
  • Futuristic concerns about AI safety, decentralized AI systems, and blockchains
  • Mechanism Design and Token Economics in blockchain
  • Decentralized Finance (DeFi) and Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO)
  • ML Model and creation copyright protection via decentralization
Reviewing will be performed in double-blind, with criteria include:
  • Quality of the methodology and experiments
  • Novelty
  • Relevance
  • Societal impacts

Author Instructions

Papers should be submitted to cmt3: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/DMLW2022

Submission Format

Submitted papers are recommended to have at most 6 pages with unlimited bibliography and appendix, using MLWEB3 2022 LaTex style files:
  • MLWEB3_2022.tex - LaTeX template
  • MLWEB3_2022.sty - style file for LaTeX 2e
  • MLWEB3_2022.pdf - example PDF output generated by running "pdflatex"
The 6-page limit is not hard, but keeping your paper reasonably short and concise can greatly improve readability. Submissions must be anonymous following NeurIPS double-blind reviewing guidelines and NeurIPS Code of Conduct. Accepted papers will be hosted on this workshop website but are considered non-archival and can be submitted to other workshops, conferences or journals if their submission policy allows.

Dual submission policy

We allow dual submission with other workshops or conferences. You can also submit papers that have some overlaps with your recently published papers. However, we disallow papers that are presented at the NeurIPS main conference as well as other machine learning conferences.
